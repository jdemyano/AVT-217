Julie Demyanovich

Producer, Composer, Animator

Bio

Ms. Julie Demyanovich is a producer, animator, composer, educator, and pilot. She is the lead researcher in a university-funded research study that investigates the relationship between developing video games and psychology for student development teams. She presented the findings at the East Coast Game Conference, the College of Humanities and Social Sciences Undergraduate Research Symposium, and George Mason University's 8th Annual Celebration of Undergraduate Research in Spring 2019.

Previously, she organized a 400-person, week-long Encampment as the cadet commander, coordinated an annual conference with over 200 in attendance, and created a leadership symposium held at the Pentagon for 40 local youth to meet high- ranking military and civilian leaders.

Ms. Demyanovich is a fourth-year college student at George Mason University where she pursues a BFA in Computer Game Design and BS in Psychology. She has served as team lead and producer for six games, including two published indie titles. Her studies have focused on integrating psychological principles with 3D user immersion through animation and sound design in Unreal and Unity.

Ms. Demyanovich has over 7 years of organizational leadership experience through volunteering with the Civil Air Patrol, the official auxiliary of the United States Air Force. In 2018, she received the Civil Air Patrol National Frank Brewer Memorial Aerospace Award, Cadet Category, for exceptional service in the advancement of STEM with a focus on aerospace education for youth during 2017.

Ms. Demyanovich currently serves as the president of the Game Analysis Design Interest Group, an organization that develops one game every semester, and actively mentors team members in sound design and music composition. She previously served as the assistant sound team director in Spring 2019.
